How this is simulated

Each analyst is a server pulling from one shared queue. Alerts, investigations, and incidents arrive as random (Poisson) events at your average monthly rate; each one's handle time is drawn around your average, not fixed — real workloads are bursty, not smooth.

Analysts aren't modeled on a real shift clock here. Instead, your hours/day, days/month, and productivity factor are folded into how much of the time each analyst is effectively available, spread continuously across the month — the same total capacity as the other two calculators, just distributed differently for this model.

This is one random realization of the month, not an average across many — click Re-run simulation to see a different possible outcome with the same inputs. That variability is the point: it's why a queue can back up even when your average utilization looks healthy.
